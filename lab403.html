Birds are living dinosaurs!

Did you know that birds are believed to be descendants of dinosaurs? This has been a highly debated topic among scientists, and dinosaurs were once believed to be giant lizards. But more recent discoveries have revealed that they actually had some bird-like tendencies.

Birds of a feather...

The discovery of feathers on dinosaurs was a big turning point in the theory of the evolution of birds. A fossil containing a feather was first discovered in 1861 belonging to the bird-like dinosaur, Archaeopteryx. Initially, a dinosaur's feathers served different functions before they evolved to be used for flight. Feathers were likely a means for warmth and display for enemies or mates during courtship. Not all dinosaurs had feathers, and not all dinosaurs evolved to be birds; however, this widely-believed theory explains that all birds evolved from dinosaurs.

Make a wish

Another key factor which points to the relationship between birds and dinosaurs is a part of their skeletal structure - the wishbone! This bone is similar to a human's collarbones but the shape of the wishbone allows birds, and at one point, dinosaurs, to fly. Because this anatomical structure belongs to no other animal, it is a very convincing piece of evidence that birds evolved from dinosaurs. Read more at Audubon.org.

There are more aspects of the skeletal structure besides the wishbone that link dinosaurs to birds:

  1. Hollow bones (for flight)
  2. Elongated arms and forelimbs
  3. Three-fingered hands, four-toed feet
  4. Large eye openings of the skull, called orbits
